## Key Concepts

### Why Sectors Matter

Not all stocks move together. When the IT sector booms, pharma might be flat. When banking struggles, FMCG might thrive. Understanding sectors — their cycles, drivers, and risks — helps you make smarter investment decisions and understand why your mutual funds behave the way they do.

India's stock market is divided into multiple sectors, but three dominate investor attention and fund manager portfolios: **Information Technology (IT)**, **Pharmaceuticals (Pharma)**, and **Banking & Financial Services (BFSI)**. Each sector has a unique character, growth engine, and risk profile.

### IT Sector — India's Global Engine

India's IT sector is the country's largest private employer and a global powerhouse. Companies like **TCS**, **Infosys**, **Wipro**, **HCL Technologies**, and **Tech Mahindra** provide software services, consulting, and digital transformation to Fortune 500 clients worldwide. The Nifty IT Index tracks 10 major IT companies.

**Key drivers:** Global technology spending, digital transformation budgets of US and European enterprises, rupee depreciation (IT earns in dollars, spends in rupees), and demand for cloud, AI, and cybersecurity services.

**Characteristics:** High profit margins (20-25% net margin), strong cash generation, generous dividends, and relatively low debt. IT companies are "asset-light" — they don't need factories or raw materials, just talented engineers. India produces 15 lakh+ engineering graduates annually, giving the sector an enormous talent pool.

**Risks:** US recession (most revenue comes from North America), visa restrictions, automation of low-end services, and rupee appreciation (reduces export earnings). The sector tends to be volatile around US Federal Reserve decisions and tech earnings seasons.

### Pharma Sector — The Prescription for Growth

India is called the "pharmacy of the world" — it supplies 20% of global generic medicines and 60% of global vaccines. Major players include **Sun Pharma**, **Dr. Reddy's**, **Cipla**, **Divi's Laboratories**, and **Lupin**. The Nifty Pharma Index tracks 10 pharma companies.

**Key drivers:** Growing healthcare spending in India (rising middle class, government health schemes like Ayushman Bharat), global generic drug demand, new drug approvals (ANDAs in the US), and biosimilar opportunities. India's cost advantage in drug manufacturing is massive — a generic drug made in India costs a fraction of the branded original.

**Characteristics:** Moderate profit margins (12-18% net margin), heavy R&D spending, regulatory complexity (FDA approvals are critical), and strong export revenue (50-60% of revenue for many companies). The sector is somewhat defensive — people need medicines regardless of economic conditions.

**Risks:** FDA warning letters or import alerts (can shut down a factory overnight), pricing pressure in the US generic market, currency fluctuation, and patent litigation. A single FDA warning letter can crash a pharma stock 10-15% in a day.

### Banking & Financial Services — The Backbone

Banking is the backbone of the Indian economy. **HDFC Bank**, **ICICI Bank**, **State Bank of India (SBI)**, **Kotak Mahindra Bank**, and **Axis Bank** dominate the sector. Financial services extend to NBFCs (Bajaj Finance, Shriram Finance), insurance (HDFC Life, SBI Life), and AMCs (HDFC AMC, Nippon India AMC).

**Key drivers:** Credit growth (loans to individuals and businesses), interest rates set by RBI, economic growth (GDP), and digital banking adoption. India's credit-to-GDP ratio is still low compared to developed economies, meaning massive room for growth.

**Characteristics:** Banks earn through the **Net Interest Margin (NIM)** — the difference between interest earned on loans and paid on deposits. Public sector banks (SBI, Bank of Baroda) have lower NIMs but massive reach. Private banks (HDFC, ICICI, Kotak) have higher NIMs, better technology, and faster growth. The sector generates huge cash flows but carries inherent risk — loans can go bad.

**Risks:** Non-performing assets (NPAs/bad loans) are the sector's biggest risk. During economic downturns, borrowers default, and banks must write off loans. The 2018 NPA crisis in India (particularly in PSU banks) wiped out billions in market value. Rising interest rates can also slow loan demand and reduce bond portfolio values.

### Sector Rotation — Timing the Shifts

Smart investors rotate between sectors based on economic cycles:
- **Early recovery:** Banks and autos benefit (cheap loans, consumer spending)
- **Mid-cycle:** IT and industrials shine (business spending picks up)
- **Late cycle:** Pharma and FMCG become defensive (people still buy medicines and soap)
- **Recession:** Pharma, FMCG, and IT (offshore revenue) hold up better

Understanding where the economy is in its cycle helps you overweight or underweight sectors. Fund managers like those managing your trust's Parag Parikh Flexi Cap (₹64,200, +42.7%) do this implicitly — shifting allocations as the economic outlook changes.
