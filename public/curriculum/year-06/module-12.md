### Building Your Investment Philosophy

As you prepare to take over this trust at 21, here's a framework for thinking like an investor:

**1. Invest for the Long Term (10+ years)**
The single most important factor in wealth creation is time. You started at 15 — by 35, you'll have 20 years of compounding behind you. Most people don't start investing until their 30s. You have a 15-year head start. Don't waste it by trading in and out of stocks.

**2. Keep Costs Low**
Every rupee paid in fees is a rupee not compounding. Prefer index funds (expense ratio 0.1-0.2%) over expensive active funds (1.5-2.5%) for your large-cap allocation. Your trust's active funds have earned their fees so far, but always evaluate: is the fund manager beating the benchmark by enough to justify the extra cost?

**3. Diversify — But Don't Over-Diversify**
Your trust's four funds cover Indian mid-cap, small-cap, flexi-cap (with international), and corporate bonds. That's solid. Adding 15 more funds doesn't reduce risk — it creates overlap and confusion. A well-built portfolio of 4-6 funds across equity and debt is all you need.

**4. Automate and Forget**
SIPs are your best friend. Set up automatic monthly investments and resist the urge to check your portfolio daily. Weekly or monthly reviews are enough. Daily checking leads to emotional decisions — you learned this in the behavioral finance module.

**5. Understand What You Own**
Never invest in something you don't understand. Before buying any stock or fund, you should be able to explain to a friend what it does, why it grows, and what could go wrong. If you can't explain it, don't buy it.

### What to Do When You Turn 21

When the trust matures and you receive ₹2,34,000+ (likely more by then, with continued SIPs), here's a practical plan:

1. **Don't rush.** You don't need to do anything on day one. The money is already invested and compounding.
2. **Review your allocation.** Is 85% equity still right for your goals? If you're starting a business or need money for education, increase debt allocation.
3. **Consider tax implications.** Long-term capital gains above ₹1.25 lakh are taxed at 12.5%. Plan redemptions to minimise tax.
4. **Start a new SIP.** Even ₹2,000-5,000 per month from your first salary will build on the foundation your trust created.
5. **Stay the course.** The same principles that grew your trust from ₹1,80,000 to ₹2,34,000 will continue to work. Patience, diversification, and discipline.
