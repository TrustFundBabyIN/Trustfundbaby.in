# Module 11: Digital Fraud & Financial Safety
**Year:** 7 (Age 19)
**Month:** 11
**Reading Time:** ~10 minutes

## Learning Objectives
- Identify the most common types of digital financial fraud in India
- Learn how to protect your bank accounts, UPI, and investment accounts
- Understand phishing, vishing, SIM swapping, and social engineering attacks
- Know what to do if you become a victim — reporting and recovery steps

## Key Concepts

### The Fraud Landscape in India
India processed over ₹20 lakh crore in UPI transactions in 2024. With this scale comes massive fraud. In FY 2023-24, Indian banks reported over 29,000 fraud cases worth ₹12,000+ crore. The most common types targeting young people:

**UPI Fraud:** Fake payment requests, QR code scams (scanning a QR code actually SENDS money, doesn't receive it), and fraudulent "payment received" screenshots. Rule: scanning a QR code is for PAYING, not receiving. If someone says "scan this QR to receive money," it's a scam. Always.

**Phishing:** Fake emails or SMS pretending to be from your bank, mutual fund company, or the Income Tax Department. They contain links to lookalike websites that steal your login credentials. The Income Tax Department will NEVER ask for your password via email. Your bank will NEVER send a link asking you to "verify your account."

**Vishing (Voice Phishing):** Phone calls pretending to be from your bank, RBI, or even the police. "Your account will be frozen unless you share your OTP." "You've won a lottery — just pay ₹5,000 processing fee." "Your KYC is expired — share your Aadhaar and PAN." The golden rule: no legitimate institution will EVER ask for your OTP, PIN, or password over the phone.

**SIM Swapping:** A fraudster convinces your mobile carrier to issue a new SIM for your number. They now receive all your OTPs. They can reset your banking passwords, authorize transactions, and empty your account. Protection: set a SIM lock/PIN with your carrier. Never share OTPs, even with people claiming to be from your bank.

**Investment Scams:** "Guaranteed 50% returns in 30 days!" "Crypto trading bot that never loses!" "Ponzi schemes disguised as mutual funds." If someone promises guaranteed high returns, it's a scam. Your trust fund has earned 42.5% over 4 YEARS — not 30 days. Real returns take time.

### The Five Commandments of Financial Safety

1. **Never share OTP, PIN, or password with ANYONE.** Not your parents, not your friends, not "bank officials." Real bank officials don't need your OTP.

2. **Use strong, unique passwords for every financial app.** Use a password manager (Bitwarden is free). Never reuse passwords across banking, email, and social media.

3. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) everywhere.** Banking apps, mutual fund platforms, email. Use authenticator apps (Google Authenticator, Authy) instead of SMS OTP when possible.

4. **Verify before you transact.** Before transferring money via UPI, verify the name shown matches the person you intend to pay. Before clicking any link, check the URL. Before downloading any app, verify the developer.

5. **Monitor your accounts regularly.** Check bank statements, credit card statements, and mutual fund portfolios weekly. Set up transaction alerts via SMS and email. Catch fraud early.

### What To Do If You're Scammed
**Step 1:** Freeze your account immediately — call your bank's fraud helpline. **Step 2:** Report to the Cyber Crime Portal ([cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)) or call 1930 (national cyber crime helpline). **Step 3:** File an FIR at your nearest police station. **Step 4:** Report to your bank in writing with all transaction details. **Step 5:** If the fraud happened in the last 24-72 hours, the bank may reverse the transaction under RBI guidelines (for unauthorized electronic transactions reported within 3 days, liability is limited).

## Real-World Example (From Your Trust)
Your trust fund's demat account holds ₹3,42,000. If a fraudster gets your login credentials through phishing, they could redeem your mutual fund units and transfer the money to their account. Protect this fiercely: use a unique, strong password for your mutual fund platform, enable 2FA, never click links in emails claiming to be from the fund house, and always log in by typing the URL directly. Your ₹3,42,000 trust took 4 years to build. A scammer can steal it in 4 minutes. Protect it like it's worth ₹3,42,000 — because it is.

## Quiz

2. What is "vishing"? — A) Video calling fraud B) Voice call phishing to steal financial info C) Virus hacking D) Virtual shopping scam (Answer: B)
3. What should you NEVER share with anyone, including "bank officials"? — A) Your name B) Your address C) OTP, PIN, or password D) Your bank's name (Answer: C)
4. Within how many hours should you report unauthorized transactions to limit liability? — A) 12 hours B) 24 hours C) 72 hours D) 7 days (Answer: C)
5. If someone promises "guaranteed 50% returns in 30 days," it's most likely: — A) A great investment opportunity B) A legitimate mutual fund C) A scam D) A government scheme (Answer: C)
