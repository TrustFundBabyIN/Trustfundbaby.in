## Key Concepts

### What Is a CIBIL Score?
Your CIBIL score is a three-digit number between 300 and 900 that represents your creditworthiness — basically, how trustworthy you are with borrowed money. Banks, NBFCs, and even some landlords and employers check this number before giving you a loan, credit card, or even a rental agreement. A score above 750 is considered good; above 800 is excellent; below 650 makes it very difficult to get any credit product.

The score is generated by TransUnion CIBIL (Credit Information Bureau India Limited), one of four RBI-licensed credit bureaus in India (others: Experian, Equifax, CRIF High Mark). When you apply for a credit card, loan, or even a phone EMI, the lender reports your behavior to these bureaus. Every payment, every missed deadline, every loan application — it all goes into your credit report.

### The Four Pillars of Your Score

**1. Payment History (35% weightage):** The single most important factor. Pay your credit card bill and EMIs on time, every time, and your score climbs. Miss one payment by 30+ days and your score can drop 50-100 points instantly. Even one late payment stays on your report for 7 years, though its impact fades over time.

**2. Credit Utilization Ratio (30%):** This is the percentage of your available credit you're actually using. If your credit card limit is ₹20,000 and you consistently spend ₹18,000, your utilization is 90% — bad. Lenders see this as dependency on credit. Aim for under 30% utilization. ₹18,000 out of ₹20,000 = 90% utilization. But ₹6,000 out of ₹20,000 = 30% — much better.

**3. Credit Age (15%):** How long you've had credit products. A 5-year-old credit card is more valuable than a 6-month-old one. This is why starting at 19 is powerful — by the time you're 25, you'll have 6 years of credit history. Most peers starting at 22-23 will have only 2-3 years.

**4. Credit Mix & Enquiries (20%):** Having a mix of credit types (credit card + personal loan + home loan) is better than just one type. And every time you apply for credit, it triggers a "hard enquiry" on your report. Too many enquiries in a short period signal desperation. Space out your applications.

### How to Check Your Score for Free
Visit [cibil.com](https://cibil.com) and request your free annual credit report. You can also check through your bank's app, Paytm, CRED, or any fintech platform. Check it at least twice a year to catch errors or fraud early. Dispute any incorrect entries — CIBIL has a dispute resolution process.

### Building Your Score from Zero at 19
Since you have no credit history yet, here's your action plan:
1. Get a secured credit card against an FD (Module 8)
2. Spend 20-30% of the limit monthly
3. Pay the FULL amount before the due date — never minimum due
4. Don't apply for multiple cards at once (each triggers a hard enquiry)
5. After 6-12 months of good behavior, apply for an unsecured card
6. Keep your oldest card active forever — it's your credit age anchor

## Real-World Example (From Your Trust)
At 19, your CIBIL score is likely "NA" (no history). By 21, when your trust matures at ₹3,42,000+ and you might want a credit card with better rewards or a small personal loan for a course, a score of 750+ (built over 2 years of disciplined credit card use) will get you approved instantly with the best interest rates. Meanwhile, your friend who never had a credit card until 23 starts from zero. You have a 4-year head start. In the credit world, age of credit history is gold. Your trust taught you about long-term investing. Your CIBIL score is the same principle applied to your financial reputation.
