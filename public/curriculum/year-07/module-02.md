## Learning Objectives

- Understand how your trust fund gains appear in your tax return

## Key Concepts

### What Is an ITR and Why File One?
An Income Tax Return is a form you submit to the Income Tax Department declaring your income, deductions, and the tax you owe (or the refund you're entitled to). It's not just for people who earn a lot — anyone whose gross income exceeds ₹2.5 lakh in a financial year (April to March) must file. Even if you earn below this threshold, filing can be beneficial: it creates a financial record, helps you get loans later, and is often required for visa applications.

For the financial year 2025-26, the due date for salaried individuals is July 31, 2026. Miss this deadline and you can still file a "belated return" until December 31, but with a late fee of up to ₹5,000 (₹1,000 if income is below ₹5 lakh). Filing on time is free and painless — procrastination costs money.

### Which ITR Form Should You Use?
The Income Tax Department has seven ITR forms, but as a 19-year-old with a part-time salary and mutual fund investments, you'll likely use one of two:

**ITR-1 (Sahaj):** For resident individuals with income up to ₹50 lakh from salary, one house property, and other sources (like interest). This is the simplest form — if your income is straightforward, this is yours.

**ITR-2:** For individuals and Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs) who don't have income from business or profession but have capital gains (which includes your mutual fund gains from the trust). When your trust matures and you realize capital gains, you'll likely graduate to ITR-2.

### How to File: The Actual Process
Filing ITR is done online at [incometax.gov.in](https://incometax.gov.in). Here's the process: (1) Log in with your PAN. (2) Select the appropriate ITR form. (3) Pre-filled data will appear — your salary from Form 16, TDS deducted, bank interest. Verify it. (4) Add any additional income (like capital gains from mutual funds). (5) Claim deductions under Section 80C, 80D, etc. (6) Calculate tax, pay any balance, or declare your refund. (7) Submit and e-verify (using Aadhaar OTP is the easiest method).

The portal has improved massively. Most salaried individuals can file in 15-20 minutes. The pre-filled data from your employer and banks does 80% of the work.

## Real-World Example (From Your Trust)
Right now, Kabir, your part-time income of ₹96,000/year is below the ₹2.5 lakh exemption. You technically don't need to file. But consider this: your trust fund has ₹1,02,000 in unrealized gains. When it matures at 21 and you redeem those mutual fund units, those gains become "realized" and must be reported. By filing voluntarily now (even a "nil return"), you create a tax history. Banks and financial institutions check ITR filing history when you apply for credit cards, loans, or even a rental apartment. A 2-year-old ITR record at age 21 puts you ahead of 99% of your peers.

## Quiz
1. What is the due date for filing ITR for salaried individuals? — A) March 31 B) July 31 C) September 30 D) December 31 (Answer: B)
2. Which ITR form is used for simple salary income up to ₹50 lakh? — A) ITR-1 B) ITR-2 C) ITR-3 D) ITR-4 (Answer: A)
3. What is the late filing fee if you miss the ITR deadline and your income is below ₹5 lakh? — A) ₹5,000 B) ₹1,000 C) ₹10,000 D) No fee (Answer: B)
4. Capital gains from mutual funds are reported in which ITR form? — A) ITR-1 only B) ITR-2 or higher C) ITR-4 D) Not required to report (Answer: B)
5. What is the easiest way to e-verify your ITR? — A) Sending physical documents B) Aadhaar OTP C) Visiting the income tax office D) Calling a helpline (Answer: B)
