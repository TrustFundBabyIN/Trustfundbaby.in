# Module 1: Your First PAN Card & Why It Matters
**Year:** 7 (Age 19)
**Month:** 1
**Reading Time:** ~10 minutes

## Learning Objectives
- Understand what a PAN card is and why every financial transaction in India requires it
- Learn how to apply for a PAN card and link it with Aadhaar
- Know when and where you must quote your PAN
- Understand the consequences of not having a PAN for financial transactions

## Key Concepts

### What Is a PAN Card?
PAN stands for Permanent Account Number. It's a unique 10-character alphanumeric identifier issued by the Income Tax Department of India to every taxpayer — and increasingly, to every financially active individual. Your PAN looks something like `ABCDE1234F`: the first five characters are letters, the next four are numbers, and the last is a letter again.

Think of PAN as your financial Aadhaar. While Aadhaar identifies you as a resident of India, PAN identifies you as a participant in India's financial system. Every time you earn money, invest, buy property, or even make a large cash transaction, the government uses your PAN to track it. This isn't surveillance — it's how India ensures tax compliance and prevents money laundering.

### When Do You Need PAN?
You need PAN far more often than most 19-year-olds realize. Here are the key situations: opening a bank account (especially a fixed deposit or demat account), receiving taxable salary, filing income tax returns, buying or selling mutual funds, purchasing property worth more than ₹10 lakh, making cash deposits above ₹50,000 in a single day, buying foreign currency, and even applying for a credit card. If your annual income exceeds ₹2.5 lakh (the basic exemption limit for individuals below 60), you are legally required to file an ITR — and for that, you need PAN.

As of recent regulations, PAN must also be linked with Aadhaar. If you don't link them, your PAN becomes "inoperative," which means you can't file returns, claim refunds, or complete any financial transaction that requires PAN verification.

### How Your PAN Connects to Your Trust Fund
Your Kabir Mehra Growth Trust was created by your parents when you were 15. But now that you're 19, your PAN becomes critical. When you turn 21 and receive the trust's maturity amount of ₹3,42,000 (current value), the capital gains — that ₹1,02,000 your money earned — will be linked to YOUR PAN. The Income Tax Department will know about this gain, and you'll need to report it in your ITR. Getting your PAN now means you're prepared for that moment.

## Real-World Example (From Your Trust)
Your trust fund has generated ₹1,02,000 in gains over 4 years. That's real money the tax department cares about. When you receive the trust at 21, equity mutual fund gains held over 1 year are taxed as Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG) at 10% (above ₹1 lakh exemption). Your gain of ₹1,02,000 means you'd owe roughly ₹200 in LTCG tax — ₹1,02,000 minus ₹1,00,000 exemption = ₹2,000 × 10%. Without a PAN, you can't even file the return to report this. Your PAN card is your ticket to being a legitimate financial adult.

Meanwhile, you're earning ₹8,000/month from your part-time job — ₹96,000/year. That's below the taxable threshold, so you won't owe tax yet. But the moment your income crosses ₹2.5 lakh (which could happen soon with a full-time job), PAN becomes mandatory.

## Quiz
1. What is the format of a PAN number? — A) 12 digits B) 10-character alphanumeric C) 8 letters and 2 numbers D) Same as Aadhaar number (Answer: B)
2. At what annual income level must you file an Income Tax Return in India? — A) ₹1,00,000 B) ₹2,50,000 C) ₹5,00,000 D) ₹10,00,000 (Answer: B)
3. What happens if you don't link PAN with Aadhaar? — A) Nothing B) You get a fine of ₹1,000 C) PAN becomes inoperative D) Aadhaar gets cancelled (Answer: C)
4. Which transaction requires PAN? — A) Buying groceries B) Cash deposit above ₹50,000 C) Paying electricity bill D) UPI transaction of ₹500 (Answer: B)
5. When your trust matures at 21, the capital gains are linked to whose PAN? — A) Your parents' PAN B) The trust's PAN C) Your PAN D) The mutual fund company's PAN (Answer: C)
