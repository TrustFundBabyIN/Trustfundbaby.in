# Module 2: PAN Card — Your Financial Identity
**Year:** 5 (Age 17)
**Month:** 2
**Reading Time:** ~10 minutes

## Learning Objectives
- Understand what a PAN card is and why every Indian needs one
- Learn how to apply for a PAN card as a minor or young adult
- Recognize PAN's role in investments, banking, and tax filing
- Know how PAN helps prevent tax evasion and financial fraud

## Key Concepts

### What Is a PAN Card?
PAN stands for **Permanent Account Number**. It's a unique 10-character alphanumeric code (like ABCDE1234F) issued by the Income Tax Department of India. Think of it as your Aadhaar card, but specifically for financial transactions. While Aadhaar proves *who you are*, PAN tracks *what you do with money*.

Every PAN number has a specific structure. The first three characters are letters from A to Z. The fourth character tells you what type of holder you are — "P" for an individual person, "C" for a company, "H" for a Hindu Undivided Family, and so on. The fifth character is the first letter of your surname (or your trust/firm's name). Then four numbers follow, and the last character is a letter that acts as a check digit.

Your PAN card is a small plastic or laminated card, but the real value is the number itself. It's linked to your identity in the Income Tax Department's database forever. Even if you lose the card, the number stays the same — hence "permanent."

### Why PAN Matters
Without a PAN card, you essentially don't exist in India's financial system. Here's where you need PAN:

**Banking:** Opening a bank account requires PAN. Any deposit or withdrawal above ₹50,000 in a single transaction triggers PAN verification.

**Investments:** Whether it's mutual funds, stocks, fixed deposits, or your Trust Fund Baby trust — PAN is mandatory. Your trust's PAN is linked to every SIP, every fund purchase, every redemption.

**Tax Filing:** You can't file an Income Tax Return (ITR) without PAN. The government uses PAN to track all your income, investments, and taxes paid.

**Property:** Buying or selling property, even as a co-owner, requires PAN.

**High-value purchases:** Buying jewellery above ₹2 lakh, a car, or making large cash deposits all need PAN.

The government uses PAN to connect the dots. If you earn ₹5 lakh, invest ₹2 lakh, and spend ₹3 lakh — PAN helps the IT Department see the full picture. This makes it harder for people to hide income or evade taxes.

### PAN for Minors — Yes, You Can Get One
Many people think PAN is only for adults. Wrong. Even a newborn can have a PAN card. For minors (under 18), a parent or guardian applies on their behalf. The minor's photo and details are used, but the guardian signs the application.

Why would a minor need PAN? Trust funds, for starters. When your parents set up your Trust Fund Baby trust, the trust itself likely has its own PAN. If you hold investments in your name (even small ones), you need PAN. Scholarship receipts above certain thresholds, prize money, or income from any source may require PAN for tracking.

Applying for PAN is straightforward. You can do it online through the NSDL or UTIITSL websites. The fee is around ₹107 (including GST) for an Indian address. You need proof of identity (Aadhaar works), proof of address, and a date of birth certificate. The PAN card arrives in about 15-20 days.

### PAN and Anti-Fraud
PAN is India's weapon against financial fraud and black money. Before PAN, people could open multiple bank accounts, buy property under different names, and hide income easily. PAN creates a single thread that connects all financial activity to one person.

If someone tries to use two different PAN numbers, it's a criminal offence — punishable by fines and even imprisonment. The Income Tax Department's systems flag duplicate PANs automatically. This is why PAN is "permanent" — you get one for life, and it tracks everything.

The government also introduced **Aadhaar-PAN linking** to strengthen this system. Since 2023, your PAN becomes "inoperative" if not linked to Aadhaar. This ensures one person = one PAN = one financial identity, connected to their biometric Aadhaar.

## Real-World Example (From Your Trust)
Kabir Mehra Growth Trust would have its own PAN number, separate from Kabir's personal PAN and his father Mr. Rajesh Mehra's PAN. Every month when the ₹5,000 SIP is processed, it's recorded under the trust's PAN. When Kabir turns 21 and the trust matures, the redemption will be processed using this PAN — and any tax on the ₹21,600 gain will be calculated and reported under it. Without PAN, none of this would be possible. It's the financial backbone of your trust.

## Quiz
1. What does PAN stand for? — A) Personal Account Number B) Permanent Account Number C) Permanent Aadhaar Number D) Private Account Network (Answer: B)
2. How many characters does a PAN number have? — A) 8 B) 9 C) 10 D) 12 (Answer: C)
3. What does the fourth character of a PAN indicate? — A) Your state B) Your birth year C) The type of holder (individual, company, etc.) D) Your bank branch (Answer: C)
4. Above what amount does a single bank transaction require PAN verification? — A) ₹10,000 B) ₹25,000 C) ₹50,000 D) ₹1,00,000 (Answer: C)
5. What happens if you hold two PAN cards? — A) Nothing, it's allowed B) You get a bonus credit score C) It's a criminal offence with fines and imprisonment D) You pay double tax (Answer: C)
