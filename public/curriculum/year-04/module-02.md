# Module 2: Your First Mutual Fund Statement
**Year:** 4 (Age 16)
**Month:** 2
**Reading Time:** ~10 minutes

## Learning Objectives
- Understand what a mutual fund statement is and why it matters to you
- Read and interpret the key sections of your trust's fund statement
- Know where your money is invested and how much each fund is worth
- Learn how to request and access your trust's statements

## Key Concepts

### What Is a Mutual Fund Statement?
A mutual fund statement is like a report card for your investments. Just like your school report card shows your marks in each subject, a mutual fund statement shows how much money is in each fund, how much you've invested, and how much profit or loss you've made. Every mutual fund company sends these statements regularly — usually monthly or quarterly.

For your trust, the statement comes from the registrar (like CAMS or KFintech) and shows all the funds your trust has invested in. It's a legal document, so it's precise — every rupee is accounted for.

### The Key Parts of Your Statement
Your mutual fund statement has several important sections. The **Folio Number** is like an account number — it identifies your trust's investment with a particular fund house. The **Fund Name** tells you which mutual fund your money is in (like HDFC Mid-Cap Opportunities Fund). **Units Held** shows how many units of the fund you own. **Current NAV** (Net Asset Value) is the price of one unit. **Current Value** is simply Units × NAV — that's what your investment is worth today. **Invested Amount** is how much money actually went in. And **Gain/Loss** is the difference between current value and invested amount.

### Why Should You Read It?
Most people — even adults — ignore their mutual fund statements. They just see a PDF in their email and delete it. But you're not most people. This is YOUR money growing. Reading your statement helps you know exactly where you stand. Is your money growing? Which fund is performing best? Are you on track? It takes about 5 minutes to read, and it gives you a complete picture of your financial health.

### How to Get Your Statement
You can get your mutual fund statement in several ways. The easiest is through the AMC (Asset Management Company) website or app — just log in with your PAN. You can also get a **Consolidated Account Statement (CAS)** from CAMS or KFintech, which shows ALL your mutual fund investments across all fund houses in one document. For your trust, since it has its own PAN (AACTK1234D), the statement will show only the trust's investments, separate from any personal investments your family might have.

## Real-World Example (From Your Trust)
Let's look at your trust's investment summary as of December 2025. Your trust has invested in four mutual funds:

- **HDFC Mid-Cap Opportunities Fund:** You invested ₹20,000. It's now worth ₹22,500. That's a gain of ₹2,500 or +12.5%.
- **SBI Small Cap Fund:** You invested ₹15,000. It's now worth ₹16,800. That's a gain of ₹1,800 or +12.0%.
- **Parag Parikh Flexi Cap Fund:** You invested ₹20,000. It's now worth ₹23,100. That's a gain of ₹3,100 or +15.5%.
- **ICICI Prudential Corporate Bond Fund:** You invested ₹10,000. It's now worth ₹10,800. That's a gain of ₹800 or +8.0%.

Your total portfolio is worth ₹73,200. You invested ₹65,000 total, and your overall gain is ₹8,200 — that's a return of 12.6%. The Parag Parikh Flexi Cap Fund is your best performer at 15.5%, while the Corporate Bond Fund is the most conservative at 8.0%.

## Quiz
1. What is a Folio Number? — A) Your bank account number B) A unique ID for your mutual fund investment with a fund house C) Your PAN number D) The NAV of your fund (Answer: B)
2. How is the Current Value of a mutual fund calculated? — A) Invested Amount + Gain B) Units × NAV C) SIP Amount × Number of Months D) Total Portfolio ÷ Number of Funds (Answer: B)
3. Which fund in Kabir's trust has the highest return? — A) HDFC Mid-Cap (+12.5%) B) SBI Small Cap (+12.0%) C) Parag Parikh Flexi Cap (+15.5%) D) ICICI Corporate Bond (+8.0%) (Answer: C)
4. What is a Consolidated Account Statement (CAS)? — A) A bank statement B) A single document showing all mutual fund investments across fund houses C) A tax filing document D) A trust deed (Answer: B)
5. Kabir's trust invested ₹65,000 total. What is the current portfolio value? — A) ₹65,000 B) ₹8,200 C) ₹73,200 D) ₹56,800 (Answer: C)
