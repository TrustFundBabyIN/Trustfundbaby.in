# Module 11: Your Trust's Annual Performance
**Year:** 4 (Age 16)
**Month:** 11
**Reading Time:** ~10 minutes

## Learning Objectives
- Learn how to evaluate your trust's performance over a full year
- Understand benchmarks and how to compare your fund's performance against them
- Calculate your trust's overall return including SIP impact
- Know what "good performance" looks like for mutual fund investments

## Key Concepts

### Annual Performance Review: Why It Matters
Once a year, you should sit down (or your trustee should) and review how the entire portfolio performed. This isn't about checking every day or panicking about market dips — it's about taking a step back and looking at the big picture. Think of it like your annual school report card, but for your money.

An annual review answers key questions: Did my portfolio grow? Which fund did best? Which underperformed? Is my asset allocation still right? Should I make any changes? This is the foundation of smart investing — not reacting to daily market noise, but making thoughtful annual decisions.

### Benchmarks: Measuring Against the Standard
Every mutual fund has a benchmark — an index that represents the type of stocks or bonds the fund invests in. For example, the HDFC Mid-Cap Opportunities Fund might benchmark against the Nifty Midcap 150 Index. If the Nifty Midcap 150 returned +14% this year and your fund returned +12.5%, the fund underperformed its benchmark by 1.5%.

Benchmarks help you understand if your fund manager is doing a good job. If a fund consistently beats its benchmark over 3-5 years, the fund manager is adding value. If it consistently trails, you might consider switching.

### Measuring Portfolio Performance
Your trust's overall performance is more important than any single fund's performance. Here's how to measure it:

**Step 1:** Total Current Value = ₹73,200
**Step 2:** Total Amount Invested = ₹65,000
**Step 3:** Total Gain = ₹73,200 - ₹65,000 = ₹8,200
**Step 4:** Overall Return = (₹8,200 ÷ ₹65,000) × 100 = 12.6%

But wait — since you invested through SIPs (not a lump sum), the actual return calculation is more nuanced. The first SIP has been invested longer than the last SIP. The XIRR method accounts for this by considering the timing of each cash flow. For your trust's first year, the XIRR might be slightly different from the simple 12.6% calculation.

### What Does "Good" Look Like?
For mutual fund investments in India, here are general benchmarks:
- **Equity funds:** 12-15% annual return is considered good
- **Debt funds:** 6-8% annual return is considered good
- **Balanced portfolio:** 10-12% annual return is solid

Your trust's 12.6% overall return is solid, especially for a first year. The equity funds performed well (12-15.5%), and the debt fund contributed steady 8%. Compared to a savings account (3-4%) or even a fixed deposit (6-7%), your trust significantly outperformed traditional options.

## Real-World Example (From Your Trust)
Let's do a full annual performance review of the Kabir Mehra Growth Trust:

**Fund-by-Fund Performance:**
| Fund | Invested | Current | Gain | Return |
|------|----------|---------|------|--------|
| HDFC Mid-Cap | ₹20,000 | ₹22,500 | ₹2,500 | +12.5% |
| SBI Small Cap | ₹15,000 | ₹16,800 | ₹1,800 | +12.0% |
| Parag Parikh Flexi | ₹20,000 | ₹23,100 | ₹3,100 | +15.5% |
| ICICI Corp Bond | ₹10,000 | ₹10,800 | ₹800 | +8.0% |
| **Total** | **₹65,000** | **₹73,200** | **₹8,200** | **+12.6%** |

**Key Observations:**
- Parag Parikh Flexi Cap is the star performer at 15.5%, likely due to its international diversification (US tech stocks had a good year)
- HDFC Mid-Cap and SBI Small Cap both returned around 12%, in line with Indian mid/small-cap market performance
- ICICI Corporate Bond at 8% provided stability — exactly what it's supposed to do
- The portfolio is well-diversified and balanced between growth and stability

**Verdict:** Excellent first year. The trust has laid a strong foundation.
