## Key Concepts

### What Is Portfolio Growth?
Your trust doesn't just hold one investment — it holds multiple mutual funds. The combined value of all these investments is called your **portfolio value**. When we talk about portfolio growth, we're talking about how this total value changes over time.

Growth happens in two ways. First, **new money enters** — every month, ₹5,000 goes into the trust via SIP, and that money buys new units. Second, **existing investments grow** — the NAV of the funds you already own goes up (or sometimes down). Both of these contribute to your portfolio growing.

For example, in Month 1, you invest ₹5,000. Your portfolio value is ₹5,000. In Month 2, you invest another ₹5,000, but your first ₹5,000 has grown to ₹5,050. Now your portfolio value is ₹10,050. Month after month, this compounds.

### Why Growth Isn't Always Linear
Here's something important: your trust's value won't go up in a straight line. Some months it might drop. Some months it might jump. This is because mutual funds invest in the stock market (for equity funds), and the stock market goes up and down based on news, economic conditions, company performance, and investor sentiment.

Imagine a graph with your portfolio value on the Y-axis and months on the X-axis. It won't be a straight line going up — it'll look more like a wavy road going uphill. The dips are scary if you look at them daily, but zoom out to a 3-5 year view and you'll see the overall direction is up.

### The Power of Starting Early
Your trust started investing when you were 13. By the time you're 21, that's 8 years of investing. The money invested in Year 1 has 8 years to grow. The money invested in Year 5 only has 4 years to grow. This is why starting early matters so much — it's not just about how much you invest, but how long it stays invested.

Let's say ₹5,000 invested at age 13 grows at 12% per year. By age 21, that single ₹5,000 becomes about ₹12,380. But ₹5,000 invested at age 18 only grows to about ₹7,800 by age 21. Same amount, different growth — all because of time.

### How to Track Growth
You can track your trust's growth in several ways. The simplest is to check your consolidated account statement every quarter and note the total value. You can also use a simple spreadsheet — columns for Date, Invested Amount, Current Value, and Return %. Over time, this spreadsheet becomes a powerful record of your trust's journey.

Apps like Groww, Zerodha Coin, or Kuvera also show growth charts automatically. For your trust, since it has a separate PAN, you can log in and see a dedicated dashboard.

## Real-World Example (From Your Trust)
Your trust has been running for 11 months (Feb 2025 to Dec 2025). Every month, ₹5,000 enters via SIP, so 11 × ₹5,000 = ₹55,000 in SIP contributions. But your total invested amount is ₹65,000. Wait — that doesn't match. Actually, the trust was created in January 2025 with an initial lump sum plus the first SIP. Regardless, your portfolio has grown from ₹0 to ₹73,200 in less than a year.

Your gains of ₹8,200 represent the money your investments earned without you doing anything extra. The funds grew because the Indian stock market had a good year — mid-cap and small-cap stocks performed well, which is why your HDFC Mid-Cap (+12.5%), SBI Small Cap (+12.0%), and Parag Parikh Flexi Cap (+15.5%) all did well. Even the safer Corporate Bond Fund contributed with +8.0%.

## Quiz
1. What are the two ways your portfolio grows? — A) Only through new SIPs B) Through new investments and existing investments increasing in value C) Only through market crashes D) Through bank interest (Answer: B)
2. Why won't your portfolio value go up in a straight line? — A) Because SIPs are irregular B) Because mutual fund values fluctuate with the market C) Because the bank charges fees D) Because trusts don't grow (Answer: B)
3. ₹5,000 invested at age 13 growing at 12% per year becomes approximately how much by age 21? — A) ₹7,000 B) ₹10,000 C) ₹12,380 D) ₹15,000 (Answer: C)
4. How many SIPs has Kabir's trust completed by December 2025? — A) 10 B) 11 C) 12 D) 13 (Answer: B)
5. What is the total gain on Kabir's trust portfolio as of December 2025? — A) ₹5,200 B) ₹6,500 C) ₹8,200 D) ₹10,000 (Answer: C)
