# Module 12: Year 1 Review & My First Trust Statement
**Year:** 1 (Age 13)
**Month:** 12
**Reading Time:** ~10 minutes

## Learning Objectives
- Review all key concepts from Year 1 in a structured way
- Understand how to read your first Trust Fund Baby trust statement
- Reflect on your financial habits and set intentions for Year 2
- Celebrate completing your first year of financial education

## Key Concepts

### Year 1 — What You've Learned
Congratulations! You've just completed a full year of financial education. Let's recap what you now know:

**Foundations (Modules 1-3):** You understand what money is (a tool for exchanging value), how to distinguish needs from wants, and the art of saving consistently. These aren't just lessons — they're life skills that most adults still struggle with.

**Planning (Modules 4-6):** You can set SMART money goals, understand how banks work, and create a basic budget using the 50-30-20 rule. You know about UPI, savings accounts, interest, and tracking expenses.

**Growth (Modules 7-11):** You know the difference between active and passive income, why investing matters, how mutual funds work, and the incredible power of compounding. You understand that starting at 13 is a superpower most people never have.

That's not a small achievement. Most 13-year-olds (and frankly, many adults) don't know half of this. You're already ahead.

### Reading Your Trust Statement
Your Trust Fund Baby trust statement is a document that shows how your trust fund is doing. Here's what to look for:

**Total Amount Invested:** This is the sum of all SIPs your parents have made so far. If they invested ₹2,000 per month for 12 months, this would be ₹24,000.

**Current Value:** This is what your investment is worth today. It might be slightly more or less than the amount invested, depending on market performance. If it's more — great, your money is growing. If it's slightly less — don't panic. Markets go up and down. You have 8 years to go.

**Units Held:** Mutual funds are measured in units. Your statement shows how many units you own. As SIPs continue, you accumulate more units over time.

**Fund Name:** Which mutual fund(s) your money is invested in. It might be an equity fund, a hybrid fund, or a mix.

**NAV (Net Asset Value):** This is the price of one unit of the mutual fund. It changes daily based on market performance. Think of it like the price per slice of your investment pizza.

### Setting Intentions for Year 2
Year 2 will go deeper — you'll learn about the stock market, different types of investments, how to read financial news, and start thinking about your own financial goals. Before moving on, reflect on these questions:

- What money habit from Year 1 have you actually started doing?
- What was the most surprising thing you learned?
- What's one financial goal you want to achieve in Year 2?
- How has your relationship with money changed?

Write your answers down. They'll be valuable to look back on when you're 21 and receiving your trust fund.

## Real-World Example
Here's what a simplified Trust Fund Baby statement might look like:

> **Trust Fund Baby Statement — Year 1**
> - Beneficiary: Aanya Sharma, Age 13
> - Trust Start Date: March 2025
> - Monthly SIP: ₹2,000
> - Total Invested: ₹24,000
> - Current Value: ₹25,440 (as of March 2026)
> - Units Held: 412.35
> - Current NAV: ₹61.70
> - Fund: HDFC Balanced Advantage Fund
> - Returns This Year: +6.0%
> - Years Until Maturity: 7 years (age 21)

Aanya invested ₹24,000 and it's already worth ₹25,440. That extra ₹1,440 is compounding starting to work. In 7 more years, this could grow significantly. The statement shows her exactly where she stands — transparent, clear, and motivating.

## Quiz
1. What is the "Total Amount Invested" on a trust statement? — A) The current value of your fund B) The sum of all SIPs made so far C) The interest earned D) The number of units you own (Answer: B)
2. If the current value is slightly less than the amount invested, should you panic? — A) Yes, sell immediately B) No, markets go up and down — you have years to go C) Yes, call the bank D) Only if it drops 50% (Answer: B)
3. NAV stands for: — A) New Account Value B) Net Asset Value C) National Average Volume D) Net Annual Variance (Answer: B)
4. Which concept did Einstein reportedly call the "eighth wonder of the world"? — A) Saving B) Budgeting C) Compounding D) Banking (Answer: C)
5. You've completed Year 1 of financial education. The most important habit to carry into Year 2 is: — A) Checking your investments every day B) Saving and investing consistently C) Spending all your money before someone else does D) Ignoring your trust statement (Answer: B)
