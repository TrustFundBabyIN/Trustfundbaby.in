# Module 7: How People Earn Money
**Year:** 1 (Age 13)
**Month:** 7
**Reading Time:** ~10 minutes

## Learning Objectives
- Understand the difference between jobs, businesses, and investments as income sources
- Learn about active income vs passive income
- Explore different careers and earning paths in India
- Start thinking about your own skills and how they could earn money someday

## Key Concepts

### Active Income: Trading Time for Money
Most people earn money by doing work — a job or a business where they actively put in time and effort. A software engineer at Infosys earns a salary. A shopkeeper in your neighbourhood earns profits from selling goods. An autorickshaw driver earns fares. A doctor earns consultation fees. All of these are active income — you work, you earn. You stop, you stop earning.

In India, the common paths to active income are: working a job (government or private), running a business or shop, freelancing (design, writing, coding), and farming. Each path has its own pros and cons. A government job offers security. A business offers unlimited upside but also risk. Freelancing offers freedom but no guaranteed income.

### Passive Income: Money While You Sleep
Passive income is money you earn without actively working for it every day. Sounds like a dream, right? It's real, but it usually requires either upfront money or upfront effort. Examples: rental income from a property your family owns, interest from a fixed deposit, dividends from shares, or royalties from a book or YouTube channel.

Here's the key insight: passive income often comes from investing money you've already earned. You earn ₹10,000 from a job, invest it, and it earns ₹1,000 on its own. That ₹1,000 is passive income. Over time, if you keep investing, your passive income can grow to match or even exceed your active income. That's the path to financial freedom.

### Multiple Streams of Income
Smart people don't rely on just one source of income. A teacher might earn a salary (active), rent out a room (passive), and invest in mutual funds (passive). Three streams. If one dries up, the others keep flowing. You don't need three streams right now at 13 — but knowing this concept early means you'll build towards it intentionally.

Think about your own skills right now. Can you tutor a younger student? Make art and sell it? Create content online? These small earnings today are more than just pocket money — they're your first taste of entrepreneurship and active income generation.

## Real-World Example
Meet the Sharma family from Hyderabad. Mr. Sharma works as an accountant (active income — ₹60,000/month). Mrs. Sharma runs a home-based tiffin service (active income — ₹25,000/month). They also own a small flat that they rent out (passive income — ₹8,000/month) and have money in mutual funds that generate returns (passive — varies). Together, four income streams support the family. When Mr. Sharma's company delayed salaries for two months, the other three streams kept the household running smoothly. That's the power of not putting all your eggs in one basket.

## Quiz
1. Active income means: — A) Money that comes without working B) Earning money by actively working C) Winning a lottery D) Inheriting money (Answer: B)
2. Which of these is passive income? — A) Salary from a job B) Profit from a shop C) Rental income from property D) Freelancing fees (Answer: C)
3. Why do smart people have multiple income streams? — A) To show off B) For financial security if one source fails C) Because one job is boring D) It's required by law (Answer: B)
4. A YouTuber who earns from old videos while sleeping is earning: — A) Active income B) Passive income C) No income D) Government salary (Answer: B)
5. Which is the BEST example of active income for a 13-year-old? — A) Pocket money from parents B) Tutoring a younger student for ₹200/week C) Interest in a savings account D) Dividends from shares (Answer: B)
