# Module 5: Introduction to Banking
**Year:** 1 (Age 13)
**Month:** 5
**Reading Time:** ~10 minutes

## Learning Objectives
- Understand what banks do and why they exist
- Learn about savings accounts and how interest works
- Know the basics of UPI, debit cards, and digital banking in India
- Understand the role of RBI as India's banking regulator

## Key Concepts

### What Does a Bank Do?
A bank is like a super-trusted locker for your money — but better. When you put money in a bank, it doesn't just sit there. The bank lends your money to other people (for homes, businesses, education) and charges them interest. Part of that interest comes back to you. So your money actually grows while it sits in the bank. That's the deal: you let the bank use your money, and they pay you for the privilege.

Banks also keep your money safe from theft, fire, or losing it under your mattress. In India, every bank deposit is insured up to ₹5 lakh by the Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation (DICGC). So even if something goes wrong with the bank (rare, but possible), your money up to ₹5 lakh is protected.

### Savings Accounts and Interest
A savings account is your first financial tool. You deposit money, and the bank pays you interest — usually around 2.5% to 7% per year depending on the bank. That means if you have ₹10,000 in your account and the interest rate is 4%, you earn ₹400 over a year just for keeping your money there. Free money? Almost.

Most Indian banks — SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Kotak — let you open a savings account as a minor (under 18) with a parent or guardian. Some banks even offer special student accounts with no minimum balance. You get an account number, a debit card (sometimes), and access to mobile banking. This is where your financial journey officially begins.

### UPI and Digital Banking
India is a global leader in digital payments, and UPI (Unified Payments Interface) is the reason. UPI lets you send and receive money instantly using just a phone number or UPI ID. Apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm use UPI. It's free, fast, and works 24/7.

As a teenager, you might already use your parents' UPI to pay at shops. Understanding that this money moves between bank accounts — not magic — is important. Every UPI transaction is recorded. Your bank statement shows everything. This transparency is a feature, not a bug. It helps you track where your money goes.

## Real-World Example
Riya, 13, opened a savings account at SBI with her mother. She deposited ₹2,000 from her birthday money. The bank gave her an account number and a passbook. Every month, she adds ₹200 from pocket money. After a year, she has ₹4,400 plus about ₹150 in interest. She checks her balance on her mother's YONO SBI app and feels proud watching the number grow. Her younger brother keeps his money in a drawer. After a year, he has... exactly what he put in. No growth. Banking beats the drawer every time.

## Quiz
1. What does a bank do with your deposited money? — A) Keeps it in a vault forever B) Lends it to others and pays you interest C) Gives it to the government D) Spends it (Answer: B)
2. What is UPI? — A) A type of bank account B) An instant digital payment system in India C) A government scheme D) A type of loan (Answer: B)
3. In India, bank deposits are insured up to: — A) ₹1 lakh B) ₹5 lakh C) ₹10 lakh D) ₹50 lakh (Answer: B)
4. If you deposit ₹10,000 at 4% annual interest, how much interest do you earn in one year? — A) ₹40 B) ₹400 C) ₹4,000 D) ₹40,000 (Answer: B)
5. Which organisation regulates banks in India? — A) SEBI B) RBI C) SBI D) NABARD (Answer: B)
