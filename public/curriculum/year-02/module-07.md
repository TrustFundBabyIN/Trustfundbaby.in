# Module 7: Staying Safe with Money (Scams & Security)
**Year:** 2 (Age 14)
**Month:** 7
**Reading Time:** ~10 minutes

## Learning Objectives
- Recognise common financial scams targeting Indians
- Learn how to protect your bank accounts and UPI PINs
- Understand phishing, SIM swap fraud, and OTP scams
- Know what to do if you become a victim of fraud

## Key Concepts

### The Scam Landscape in India
As India's digital payments have exploded, so have digital scams. In 2023, Indians lost thousands of crores to online fraud. The cruel irony is that the same technology that makes payments convenient (UPI, online banking, cards) also creates new opportunities for criminals.

But here's the thing — most scams don't succeed because of fancy hacking. They succeed because of human error. Scammers trick you into giving them your OTP, your PIN, or your password. They don't break into your bank account — they get you to open the door for them. That's why being aware is your best defence.

Let's walk through the most common scams so you can spot them from a mile away.

### Common Scams You Must Know
**1. The "Your Account Will Be Blocked" Scam**: You get an SMS or call saying your bank account or Aadhaar will be deactivated unless you "verify" immediately. They give you a link to a fake website that looks exactly like SBI or HDFC's site. You enter your details, and they have your login. Rule: Banks NEVER ask you to verify your account via SMS links. Never click links in unsolicited messages. If worried, call your bank's official number (printed on your debit card, not given in the message).

**2. OTP Fraud**: Someone calls pretending to be from your bank, Paytm, or even the police. They say there's a problem with your account and ask for the OTP that was just sent to your phone. The moment you share that OTP, they complete a transaction using your account. Rule: OTP is your digital signature. NOBODY — not bank staff, not police, not your best friend — should ever see your OTP.

**3. SIM Swap Fraud**: A scammer gets your phone number deactivated (by visiting your mobile operator with fake documents) and activates a new SIM on their phone. Now they receive your OTPs. They use this to access your bank account. Rule: If your phone suddenly loses signal and doesn't recover in a few minutes, call your mobile operator immediately. Set up a SIM lock/PORT PIN with your operator.

**4. QR Code Scams**: Someone sends you a QR code saying "scan this to receive money." But scanning a QR code and entering your UPI PIN actually SENDS money from your account. Rule: You only need to enter your PIN to SEND money, never to receive it. If someone asks you to scan a QR code and enter your PIN to "receive" money, it's a scam.

**5. Investment Scams**: "Double your money in 30 days!" "Guaranteed 50% returns!" These schemes promise impossible returns to lure you in. They might pay initial investors using new investors' money (this is called a Ponzi scheme) until the whole thing collapses. Rule: If returns sound too good to be true, they are. No legitimate investment guarantees high returns. Stick to regulated platforms.

### How to Protect Yourself
Think of your financial security like locking your house:

**Strong passwords**: Use unique, complex passwords for banking apps. Never use your birthday or "123456." Use a password manager if possible.

**Two-factor authentication (2FA)**: Enable it everywhere. Even if someone gets your password, they still need your OTP or biometric to log in.

**Screen lock**: Always have a PIN, pattern, or biometric lock on your phone. If your phone is stolen, this is your first line of defence.

**Transaction alerts**: Turn on SMS and email alerts for all bank transactions. If someone makes an unauthorised transaction, you'll know immediately and can block your card/account.

**Verify before you pay**: Before sending money via UPI, double-check the name that appears. Scammers create UPI IDs that look similar to legitimate ones.

**Report immediately**: If you've been scammed, call your bank's fraud helpline AND the national cybercrime helpline (1930) immediately. The faster you report, the higher the chance of recovering your money. You can also file a complaint at cybercrime.gov.in.

## Real-World Example
In 2023, a widely reported scam involved fake "customer care" numbers on Google. When people searched for "Paytm customer care" or "SBI helpline," Google ads showed fake numbers at the top of results. People called these numbers, thinking they were reaching their bank or wallet. The scammers, posing as customer service, would ask for OTPs and drain accounts. Google eventually cracked down, but not before thousands lost money. Lesson: Always find customer care numbers from the official app or website — never from a Google search or social media.

## Quiz
1. What should you NEVER share with anyone, including bank staff? — A) Your name B) Your UPI PIN or OTP C) Your bank's name D) Your city (Answer: B)
2. What is the national cybercrime helpline number in India? — A) 100 B) 1930 C) 1091 D) 1800 (Answer: B)
3. If someone asks you to scan a QR code and enter your PIN to "receive" money, what's happening? — A) You're receiving money B) You're actually sending money — it's a scam C) The QR code is broken D) You need a different app (Answer: B)
4. What is a Ponzi scheme? — A) A government savings plan B) A scam that pays old investors with new investors' money until it collapses C) A type of fixed deposit D) A stock trading strategy (Answer: B)
5. What should you do immediately if your phone suddenly loses all signal? — A) Restart the phone B) Wait for it to recover C) Call your mobile operator — it could be SIM swap fraud D) Buy a new phone (Answer: C)
