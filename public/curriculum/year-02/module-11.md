# Module 11: Don't Put All Eggs in One Basket (Diversification)
**Year:** 2 (Age 14)
**Month:** 11
**Reading Time:** ~10 minutes

## Learning Objectives
- Understand what diversification means and why it matters
- Learn how to spread investments across different asset types
- Know the concept of correlation in investing
- Understand how mutual funds provide instant diversification

## Key Concepts

### The Egg Basket Problem
"Never put all your eggs in one basket." You've probably heard this old saying. In investing, it's one of the most important rules to follow.

Here's why: imagine you invest all ₹1,00,000 in a single company's shares. If that company does brilliantly, you make great returns. But if that company faces a crisis — a fraud scandal, a failed product, a government ban — your entire ₹1,00,000 could crash to ₹20,000 or even zero. You've lost 80–100% of your investment because you bet everything on one horse.

Now imagine you split that ₹1,00,000 across 20 different companies in different industries. If one company fails and its shares go to zero, you lose ₹5,000 — painful, but your remaining ₹95,000 is still working for you. The damage is contained. That's diversification in action.

Diversification doesn't guarantee you'll make money. But it dramatically reduces the chance of catastrophic loss. It's the investing equivalent of wearing a seatbelt — you hope you never need it, but you're glad it's there when something goes wrong.

### Diversifying Across Asset Types
True diversification isn't just buying shares of different companies. It's about spreading money across different **types** of investments, called asset classes. Different asset classes behave differently in different conditions:

**Equity (Stocks)**: High growth potential, high risk. Best for long-term wealth building. When the economy is booming, stocks tend to do well.

**Debt (Bonds, FDs, RDs)**: Lower returns, lower risk. Provides stability and regular income. When stock markets crash, good quality bonds often hold their value or even go up.

**Gold**: Acts as a safety net during crises. When everything else is falling, gold often rises because people rush to "safe" assets. Indians have an emotional and cultural connection to gold — and it turns out, it's also a smart diversification tool.

**Real Estate**: Property investments that provide rental income and potential appreciation. Less liquid (harder to sell quickly) but tangible.

**Cash and Liquid Funds**: Money in savings accounts or liquid mutual funds. Lowest returns but immediately accessible for emergencies.

A well-diversified portfolio might look like: 60% equity (stocks/mutual funds), 20% debt (FDs/bonds), 10% gold, 10% cash. The exact percentages depend on your age, goals, and risk tolerance. Since you're 14 and your Trust Fund Baby investment has 7 years to grow, a higher allocation to equity makes sense — you have time to ride out any temporary crashes.

### Correlation — Why Different Assets Move Differently
**Correlation** is a fancy word that describes how two things move in relation to each other. If two investments always go up and down together, they have high correlation. If one goes up when the other goes down, they have low or negative correlation.

Ideally, you want investments with low correlation in your portfolio. Here's why: if stocks and gold tend to move in opposite directions (when stocks fall, gold often rises), then having both in your portfolio means that when one part goes down, the other part might go up, cushioning your overall loss.

In reality, during extreme panic (like COVID in March 2020), almost everything falls together — stocks, bonds, even gold initially. But over normal market conditions, different assets do behave differently, and that's what makes diversification work.

### How Mutual Funds Make Diversification Easy
Remember from Year 1, a mutual fund pools money from many investors and invests in a basket of securities. This is essentially built-in diversification.

An equity mutual fund might hold shares of 50–100 different companies. A balanced or hybrid fund holds both stocks and bonds. A multi-asset fund holds stocks, bonds, and gold. By investing in one mutual fund, you're automatically diversified across many investments.

This is exactly why SIPs in mutual funds are such a powerful tool for young investors. You don't need ₹1,00,000 to diversify. With a ₹500 SIP, you get exposure to dozens of companies, managed by a professional fund manager, with automatic diversification built in. Your Trust Fund Baby SIP is doing this for you right now.

## Real-World Example
Think about what happened in 2023–2024 in India. While the Nifty 50 (large company stocks) gave moderate returns, small-cap stocks surged 50–60%. At the same time, gold prices hit record highs (rising over 20% in a year). Meanwhile, FD interest rates increased because the RBI raised the repo rate. An investor who had money only in large-cap stocks missed the small-cap and gold rally. An investor diversified across large-caps, small-caps, gold, and FDs captured gains from multiple asset classes. Diversification ensured they didn't miss out, regardless of which part of the market was doing well.

## Quiz
1. What does "diversification" mean in investing? — A) Investing all your money in one stock B) Spreading investments across different assets to reduce risk C) Buying only the cheapest stocks D) Investing only in gold (Answer: B)
2. Why is it risky to invest all your money in one company? — A) The company might do too well B) If that company fails, you could lose most or all of your investment C) Diversification is more fun D) You'll pay more taxes (Answer: B)
3. Which asset class is often considered a "safe haven" during market crises? — A) Small-cap stocks B) Cryptocurrency C) Gold D) Penny stocks (Answer: C)
4. What does "low correlation" between two investments mean? — A) They always move together B) They tend to move independently or in opposite directions C) They have the same returns D) They are in the same industry (Answer: B)
5. How does investing in a mutual fund help with diversification? — A) It only invests in one company B) It automatically invests in a basket of many securities C) It guarantees profits D) It eliminates all risk (Answer: B)
