## The Simplest Wealth Formula

Compound interest means earning interest on your interest. It starts slow, then explodes.

**Year 1:** You invest ₹1,00,000. At 12% return, you earn ₹12,000.
**Year 2:** You now have ₹1,12,000. 12% of that is ₹13,440.
**Year 10:** Your ₹1,00,000 has become ₹3,10,585.
**Year 20:** ₹9,64,629.
**Year 30:** ₹29,95,992.

You invested ₹1,00,000 once. Time did the rest.

## The Two Variables That Matter

### 1. Time

Starting at 25 vs 35 is not a 10-year difference. It's often a 50%+ difference in final corpus. Every year you delay costs lakhs.

### 2. Consistency

A SIP of ₹5,000/month for 30 years at 12% = ₹1.76 crores.
The same ₹5,000/month for 20 years = ₹50 lakhs.

That 10 extra years? Worth ₹1.26 crores.

## The Child Advantage

When you start investing for a child at birth, you have an 18-year runway. That's the longest compounding horizon most families will ever have.

### Comparison: Start at Birth vs Start at Age 10

| Monthly SIP | Start Age | End Age | Final Corpus |
|-------------|-----------|---------|--------------|
| ₹5,000 | 0 | 18 | ₹34.2 lakhs |
| ₹5,000 | 10 | 18 | ₹8.7 lakhs |

Same amount invested. Four times the difference. That's the power of time.

## Don't Wait for "More Money"

The biggest mistake is waiting until you can invest more. ₹1,000/month started today beats ₹5,000/month started five years from now.

Start small. Start now. Let compound interest do the heavy lifting.
